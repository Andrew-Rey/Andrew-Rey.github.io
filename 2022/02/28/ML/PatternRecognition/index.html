Where ωi(i=1,…,c)\omega_i(i = 1,\dots, c)ωi​(i=1,…,c) is the category of research object. x\mathbf{x}x is the feature vector, has ddd dimensions. We call P(ωi∣x)P(\omega_i | \mathbf{x})P(ωi​∣x) posterior, P(ωi)P(\omega_i)P(ωi​) prior probability and p(x∣ωi)p(\mathbf{x} | \omega_i)p(x∣ωi​) likelihood of x\mathbf{x}x in category ωi\omega_iωi​. Bayesian Decision Theory: In order to minimize the total risk RRR, for i=1,…,ai = 1, \dots, ai=1,…,a we calculate the class-conditional probability

Maximum Likelihood estimation and Bayesian estimation

In the chapter 2, we suppose the likelihood function p(x∣ω)p(x|\omega)p(x∣ω) is known, but it impossible to know about it in the daily life or experiments, so we must estimate the likelihood function. Generally speaking, we have two methods to solve this problem: the one is parameter estimation and the other is non-parameter estimation.

In parameter estimation, we will give a prior model of likelihood function of xxx with unknown parameters, and what we should do is estimating the parameters using the data set.
